Ten Tors folklore

It started right on this route. In September 1959, three Army officers — Col Gregory, Maj Parker and Capt Joyner — were watching their Junior Leaders bivouac near Sittaford Tor (checkpoint 6 on Route I) and asked: why should this only be for cadets? The first public Ten Tors followed in September 1960 with ~200 youths. By 1975 Sussex Police Cadets became the first girls' team to finish the 45-mile route, and the Challenge has been the spring rite of Dartmoor ever since.
